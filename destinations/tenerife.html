Flights from London to Tenerife: Complete Guide to Airlines, Prices & Travel Tips

Tenerife is widely known as the "Island of Eternal Spring," making it a perennial favorite for UK holidaymakers seeking reliable sunshine without the long-haul travel time. The route from London to Tenerife is incredibly popular, especially during the UK's colder months, as travelers flock to the Canary Islands for its volcanic landscapes, diverse beaches, and vibrant resorts. With a flight time that allows you to have breakfast in London and lunch by the pool, and frequent departures from all major London airports, Tenerife remains one of the most accessible and attractive destinations for families, couples, and winter sun seekers alike.

Overview of Flights from London to Tenerife

The air route between London and Tenerife covers approximately 1,800 miles (2,900 km). Direct flights typically take between 4 hours 15 minutes and 4 hours 45 minutes. It is classified as a medium-haul flight, comfortably served by narrow-body aircraft like the Airbus A320 and Boeing 737 families. Due to high demand, there are dozens of flights daily, with early morning departures being popular to maximize time on the island.

London Airports Serving Tenerife Flights

London is exceptionally well-connected to Tenerife, with flights available from nearly all major airports: London Heathrow (LHR): Primarily served by British Airways. This is the best option for travelers seeking a full-service experience or connecting from other long-haul flights. London Gatwick (LGW): A massive hub for leisure travel. You'll find frequent daily departures from British Airways, easyJet, and TUI Airways. It serves as the main gateway for many package holiday travelers. London Stansted (STN): The key base for low-cost carriers in the north of London. Ryanair and Jet2 operate extensively from here, offering competitive fares and high frequency. London Luton (LTN): Another excellent option for budget travelers north of the capital, with regular services from easyJet, Ryanair, and TUI.

Tenerife Airports for UK Travelers

Tenerife has two airports, and choosing the right one can save you significant transfer time: Tenerife South (TFS - Reina Sofía): This is the main international airport, handling the vast majority of UK tourist traffic. It is conveniently located near the most popular holiday resorts, including Playa de las Américas, Los Cristianos, and Costa Adeje. Tenerife North (TFN - Ciudad de La Laguna): Located near the capital, Santa Cruz. While it primarily serves inter-island and mainland Spain flights, it is closer to Puerto de la Cruz and the lush northern hiking trails. Double-check your arrival airport before booking accommodation.

Airlines Flying from London to Tenerife

Travelers can choose from a mix of flag carriers, low-cost airlines, and charter services: British Airways: Offers both Economy (Euro Traveller) and Business (Club Europe) cabins. Ideal for those who want complimentary snacks/water and a more generous hand luggage allowance on standard fares. easyJet: A major operator on this route, known for reliable service and competitive pricing. Standard fares are seat-only, with bags and seat selection as extras. Ryanair: Often offers the lowest headline fares, especially from Stansted and Luton. Perfect for budget-conscious travelers who travel light. Jet2: Highly rated for customer service and generous baggage policies (often 22kg included in package holidays). They fly from Stansted and offer a very family-friendly experience. TUI Airways: A charter airline that also sells flight-only tickets. They often use larger aircraft like the Boeing 787 Dreamliner on this route during peak season, offering seat-back entertainment.

Direct vs Connecting Flights

Direct Flights: This is the standard and most recommended way to travel. With flight times under 5 hours and abundant availability, there is rarely a need to connect. Connecting Flights: While you can fly via Madrid or Barcelona with Iberia or Vueling, this often doubles the travel time to 8-10 hours. Connecting flights are generally not cheaper than direct low-cost options and should only be considered if direct flights are sold out or exceptionally expensive.

London to Tenerife Flight Prices (Economy Class)

Prices can fluctuate wildly depending on the time of year: Low Season (Nov, Jan, Feb - excluding half-term): Return tickets can be found for as little as £40 - £80 with budget carriers. Mid-Range: Standard prices for booking a few months out usually sit between £100 and £200 return. Peak Season (Christmas, Easter, August, School Holidays): Prices spike significantly. Expect to pay £300 - £600+ for a return ticket if you don't book well in advance.

Best Time to Book Cheap Flights

Book Early: For school holiday dates (especially Christmas and February half-term), book as soon as flights are released (typically 11 months in advance for scheduled airlines). The "Sweet Spot": For non-holiday dates, booking 1 to 3 months in advance often yields the best balance of price and availability. Winter Sun Demand: Remember that Tenerife is a prime winter destination. Unlike European city breaks where winter is cheaper, Tenerife prices stay robust in January and February due to the high demand for warmth.

Travel Tips for London–Tenerife Flights

Seat Selection: If you are traveling as a group or family on a low-cost airline (Ryanair/easyJet), paying for seat selection is the only way to guarantee you sit together. Transfers: If you land at Tenerife South (TFS) but are staying in the north (Puerto de la Cruz), the transfer can take 60-90 minutes. Ensure you book a car rental or shuttle in advance. Food & Drink: Most low-cost carriers charge for water and food. Bring an empty water bottle to fill up at London airports after security, and pack some snacks for the 4.5-hour journey.

Entry Requirements for UK Travelers

Passport Rules: Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months after the day you plan to leave Tenerife, and it must be less than 10 years old on the date you enter. Visa-Free Travel: UK citizens can visit Spain (and the Canary Islands) for up to 90 days in any 180-day period without a visa for tourism. Customs Differences: The Canary Islands are outside the EU's VAT area. Duty-free allowances for alcohol and tobacco are often more generous than mainland Spain, but strict limits apply when bringing goods back into the UK. Check the latest government advice before purchasing.

Conclusion

Whether you choose the premium service of British Airways from Heathrow or a value-packed deal with Ryanair from Stansted, getting from London to Tenerife is straightforward and convenient. With year-round sunshine waiting at the other end, the 4-hour journey is a small price to pay for a slice of paradise. By booking smart and choosing the right airport for your needs, you can start your Canary Islands holiday smoothly and stress-free.
